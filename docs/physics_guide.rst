Physics Guide
=============

Energy Wave Theory (EWT) Implementation
----------------------------------------

OpenWave implements Energy Wave Theory, which proposes that all matter and energy arise from quantum wave interactions in a granular spacetime medium.

Fundamental Concepts
--------------------

Quantum Waves
~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The foundation of EWT is the quantum wave, characterized by:

- **Wavelength**: Planck length (λₚ = 1.616255 × 10⁻³⁵ m)
- **Amplitude**: Related to energy density
- **Frequency**: Determined by wave interactions

Granular Spacetime
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Spacetime is modeled as a lattice of quantum granules with:

- **Granule spacing**: Planck length
- **Granule mass**: Corrected Planck mass (see scientific_source/a4)
- **Wave propagation**: Speed of light through the medium

Particle Formation
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Particles form from standing wave patterns:

- **Electrons**: Stable standing wave configurations
- **Protons**: Complex multi-wave structures
- **Neutrons**: Composite wave patterns

Key Equations
-------------

The simulator implements equations from the EWT papers:

1. **Energy Wave Equation**: E = A²ωc
2. **Force Equations**: F = (K₁K₂/r²) × amplitude factors
3. **Wave Interference**: Constructive and destructive patterns

For complete equation documentation, see the Constants and Equations reference (scientific_source/a6).

Validation
----------

The simulation validates against:

- Particle masses and charges
- Force calculations at various scales
- Energy conservation principles
- Experimental observations
